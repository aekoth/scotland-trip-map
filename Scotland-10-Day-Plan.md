# Scotland — Tentative 10-Day Plan 🏴

**Late October • Two bases: Inverness (north) → Edinburgh (south)**

The idea: settle into Inverness for the Highlands, road-trip south on Day 5 (hitting the good stuff in the middle), then settle into Edinburgh for the city. Only two hotels the whole trip.

**Our confirmed favorites:** Loch Ness · one Highland horse ride · Blair Castle tour · whisky tour · Edinburgh Castle (from outside) · Holyrood Palace tour · the make-your-own-bag workshop · the volcano hike (Arthur's Seat)

**How to read this:** each day has a suggested plan with rough hours, plus *Swap options* — anything in there can replace the plan for that day. Nothing's locked. Times are loose and assume an easy pace, not a march.

---

## NORTH — Inverness (4 nights)

### Day 1 — Arrive & settle
- **Land in Inverness, pick up the rental car** — give yourself a slow evening.
- **Stroll the Ness Islands** *(~1 hr)* — little footbridges over the river, a gentle leg-stretch after travel.
- Easy dinner in town.

*Swap options:* skip the stroll entirely and just rest; or an early dinner out if you land with energy.

### Day 2 — Loch Ness
- **Drive down Loch Ness** *(~30 min each way)* — the classic Highland view.
- **Urquhart Castle viewpoint / grounds** *(~1 hr)* — it's a dramatic ruin right on the water (not a furnished one, so no need to pay in unless you want to — the lochside setting is the draw).
- **Loch Ness boat cruise** *(~1 hr)* — best way to actually be *on* the loch.

*Swap options:* drive further to Fort Augustus and watch boats climb the canal locks; or skip the cruise and do a lochside walk.

### Day 3 — Horse ride in the Cairngorms
- **Drive to Aviemore** *(~45 min each way)* — the heart of Highland pony trekking.
- **Horse trek** *(1–2 hrs in the saddle)* — riding through ancient pine forest and heather with mountain views. *Book ahead; pick the route length when you book.*
- **Hairy Coo Safari + Calves & Coos, Rothiemurchus** *(~1–2 hrs)* — a ranger calls the Highland cows right up to the fence for hand-feeding (~£14–20pp), and the Calves & Coos upgrade adds a Land Rover trip to bottle-feed the orphan calf. Five minutes from Aviemore — pairs perfectly with the trek. *Book ahead.*
- **Time in the Cairngorms** *(remainder)* — easy forest walks, or the mountain funicular.

*Swap options:* longer ride; more Rothiemurchus estate activities (trails, lochs, wildlife); or the Cairngorm funicular for big views without the climb.

### Day 4 — Highlands flex day
- **Culloden Battlefield** *(~1.5–2 hrs)* — the 1746 battlefield, very moving; big for *Outlander* fans.
- **Clava Cairns** *(~30 min)* — 4,000-year-old standing stones, right next door.

*Swap options:* **Glen Affric** for a half-day of Scotland's prettiest glen scenery; **Chanonry Point** for wild dolphin-watching from shore; or an ambitious long day to the **Isle of Skye** (~2.5 hrs each way — only if you're up for a big drive).

## DRIVE DAY — Inverness to Edinburgh

### Day 5 — South through the middle *(full day, ~3.5 hrs driving + stops)*
- **Drive to Pitlochry** *(~1 hr 45 min)*.
- **Whisky tour — Edradour or Blair Athol distillery** *(~1–1.5 hrs)* — your one alcohol tour, right on the route. *Book ahead.*
- **Blair Castle tour** *(~1.5–2 hrs)* — the fully-furnished, white-turreted family castle, 15 min past Pitlochry. The furnished one you've been waiting for. *Book ahead.*
- **Drive on to Edinburgh** *(~1 hr 45 min)* — **drop the rental car** when you arrive; you won't need it in the city.

*Swap options:* swap the distillery for **Dewar's Aberfeldy** nearby; add a quick photo stop at **the Kelpies** (giant horse-head sculptures) near the end of the drive if energy allows; or skip the castle here and bank the time for Edinburgh (but this is the day Blair fits best).

> ⚠️ **Check your dates against Blair Castle.** Blair's 2026 season ends **October 30** — after that it's closed for winter (limited pre-booked tours only). And in the final week of October it closes earlier, with **last entry as early as 2pm**. So: make sure the drive day lands **on or before Oct 30**, leave Inverness in the morning, and aim to reach Blair by **early afternoon**. If your dates fall after Oct 30, swap in **Stirling Castle** (open year-round, with furnished royal apartments) as the drive-day castle instead.

## SOUTH — Edinburgh (5 nights)

### Day 6 — Old Town & the volcano
- **Royal Mile walk + Edinburgh Castle from the esplanade** *(~1–1.5 hrs)* — the views and the exterior are free; soak up the icon without the crowds inside.
- **Palace of Holyroodhouse tour** *(~1.5–2 hrs)* — the King's furnished Edinburgh residence at the foot of the Mile, with Mary Queen of Scots' chambers. *Your furnished city castle.*
- **Arthur's Seat hike** *(~1.5–2 hrs)* — the extinct volcano in the middle of the city; ~45 min up to a 360° view. Right behind Holyrood, so it pairs perfectly.

*Swap options:* swap the hike for gentle **Calton Hill** *(~30–45 min)* if you'd rather an easy climb; add an **underground vaults / ghost tour** in the evening.

### Day 7 — Make-your-own-bag + museum
- **Islander bag workshop** *(~1 hr)* — design and build your own Harris Tweed + leather bag (satchel or backpack). *Book ahead — very popular.*
- **National Museum of Scotland** *(~2–3 hrs)* — free and genuinely world-class; great if the weather turns.
- Wander the Old Town, shops, and cafés in between.

*Swap options:* **Scotch Whisky Experience** by the castle if you want a second tasting; **Dean Village + Water of Leith walk** for storybook-pretty riverside; **Stockbridge** for indie shops and food.

### Day 8 — Day trip
- **Pick one:**
  - **Rosslyn Chapel** *(half day; ~30 min out)* — the intricately carved *Da Vinci Code* chapel.
  - **North Berwick** *(full day; ~40 min out)* — seaside town, a small volcanic hill to climb, and a seabird center.
  - **Glasgow** *(full day; ~1 hr by train)* — bigger city, the free and excellent Kelvingrove museum.

*Swap options:* or stay in Edinburgh and go deeper — Holyrood Park, the Botanic Garden, or more museums.

### Day 9 — Edinburgh flex & loose ends
- **Calton Hill** *(~45 min)* — the most photographed view in the city.
- **Whatever you loved you want more of** — a second museum, last shopping, a long lunch, the bits Day 6–8 didn't fit.

*Swap options:* a relaxed spa/cafe day; revisit a favorite; or save a day-trip option from Day 8 you couldn't fit.

### Day 10 — Departure
- Fly out of Edinburgh. Allow time to get to the airport (~30 min from the center).

## Quick booking checklist

**Book first — these sell out or set your dates:**
- [ ] Flights — into **Inverness**, out of **Edinburgh** (Inverness is a small airport, so you'll likely connect via a UK/EU hub)
- [ ] Hotel 1 — **Inverness**, 4 nights
- [ ] Hotel 2 — **Edinburgh**, 5 nights
- [ ] Rental car — **one-way, Inverness → Edinburgh**; reserve an **automatic** if you don't want a manual

**Book ahead — timed tickets / very popular:**
- [ ] Blair Castle — *mind the season note on Day 5*
- [ ] Holyroodhouse
- [ ] Islander bag workshop
- [ ] Horse trek (Cairngorms / Aviemore)
- [ ] Whisky tour (Pitlochry)
- [ ] Loch Ness cruise (optional)
- [ ] Hairy Coo Safari + Calves & Coos bottle-feeding (Rothiemurchus)

**No booking needed:** Edinburgh Castle (you're only seeing it from outside) · National Museum of Scotland (free) · Arthur's Seat · Calton Hill

**If you swap any of these in, they need booking too:** Rosslyn Chapel · an underground / ghost tour · the Scotch Whisky Experience · the Cairngorm funicular · any North Berwick boat trip.

*Late-October note: book activities a week or two out — daylight is short (afternoon sunsets, and the clocks go back at month's end) and some places run reduced autumn hours or close for the season entirely.*

## Packing list (late October — expect rain, wind & short days)

**Weather essentials**
- [ ] Waterproof jacket each — a proper hooded rain shell (your "umbrella," since wind kills real umbrellas)
- [ ] Rain trousers — mainly for the horse ride, Arthur's Seat, and any Highland walks
- [ ] Warm mid-layers — fleece or sweater, plus a thermal base layer
- [ ] Hat, gloves, scarf
- [ ] Quick-dry trousers (skip jeans on active days — they stay cold and wet)

**Footwear**
- [ ] Waterproof walking shoes or light boots, broken in — for cobblestones, the volcano hike, and castle grounds
- [ ] Comfortable everyday shoes
- [ ] Something nicer for dinners (optional)

**For the horse ride**
- [ ] Long trousers + closed shoes with a small heel — helmets and gear are usually provided, so only bring riding boots if you already own them

**Driving**
- [ ] Driver's license (+ an International Driving Permit if your country requires one)
- [ ] Phone mount and a charging cable for the car; download offline maps before you go
- [ ] Remember: driving is on the **left**, and you reserved an **automatic**

**Tech & practical**
- [ ] UK plug adapters (Type G — three rectangular pins)
- [ ] Power bank — cold + lots of photos drains phones fast
- [ ] Chargers, camera

**Day-to-day**
- [ ] Packable daypack for hikes and day trips
- [ ] Reusable water bottle
- [ ] Lip balm / moisturizer (the wind is drying), any meds, small first-aid kit
- [ ] Sunglasses — the low autumn sun is blinding when it does come out

**Documents & money**
- [ ] Passports + travel insurance details
- [ ] A contactless card — lots of places are **card-only** now, including some castle ticket desks and cafés; cash is optional

**You can skip:** heavy-duty midge repellent (the midges are gone by late October) and a big umbrella (the hood wins).
